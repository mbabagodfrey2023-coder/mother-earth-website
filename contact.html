// QUICK ANSWERS

Common Questions

Before writing to us, see if your question is answered here.

How quickly do you actually respond?

Standard enquiries: within 48 business hours. Media deadlines and investor enquiries: within 24 hours. If you haven't heard back in 48 hours, send a follow-up — sometimes messages go to spam. Use the channel email addresses directly for the fastest route.

I'm a developer — where do I get API access?

API access is available via our IRIS Marketplace. Guardian ($99/mo) gives read access to live environmental data. Sentinel ($499/mo) adds full API + analytics. Planetary ($1,499/mo) is the full platform suite with custom SLA. Contact us via the general channel to discuss custom enterprise terms.

Can I cite Mother Earth data in my research?

Yes. All published data is available under our MEDataLicense v1.0. Academic and non-commercial research use is free with attribution. For commercial research, a licence agreement is required. Email hello@motherearth.systems with your institution and intended use.

How do I become a Guardian?

Send an email to hello@motherearth.systems with the subject "Citizen Guardian Application". Tell us your name, country, and how you'd like to contribute — data monitoring, community outreach, translation, research, or any other skill. The Guardian community is voluntary and open to everyone.

What documents do investors receive?

We share freely: Investment Policy Statement, Purpose Trust Summary, MEDataLicense v1.0, and IRIS Terms of Service. On request for serious investors: full Trust Deed (16 clauses + 4 schedules), financial model, agent architecture documentation, and the VASP Act 2025 compliance summary. Email hello@motherearth.systems with "Investor Document Pack" in the subject line.

Are you accepting chapter applications right now?

Yes. We have open chapter slots in West Africa, Southern Africa, South Asia, Southeast Asia, Latin America, and Northern Europe. Each chapter requires a committed lead organisation and at least one human chapter director. See the Chapters page for current openings, requirements, and application process.
