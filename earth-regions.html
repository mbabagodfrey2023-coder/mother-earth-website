Global Ice Loss

Earth is losing 700 billion tonnes of ice every year — from Greenland, Antarctica, and mountain glaciers combined. This is the single largest driver of sea level rise and a key amplifier of warming through albedo feedback.

Arctic sea ice is already floating — so its loss doesn't directly raise sea level. But it's the planet's air conditioner: the white surface reflects 80% of solar energy back to space. As it disappears, dark ocean absorbs that heat instead, warming the Arctic 4× faster than the global average. This albedo feedback loop is self-accelerating.

Global Forest Destruction

37 million acres of forest disappear every year — 1 football pitch every 1.2 seconds. Tropical deforestation alone releases 3 billion tonnes of CO₂ annually, equal to the entire European Union's emissions. These aren't distant statistics. They're happening right now.

// OCEAN · GLOBAL SEA LEVEL RISE

Global Sea Level Rise

The global ocean has risen 21 centimetres since 1900. The rate is now 3.4mm per year and accelerating. 800 million people live in coastal zones that will be permanently flooded within this century. The number you see below is exact.

🏝️ PACIFIC ISLANDS
5.1 mm/yr
1.5× global average · thermal expansion dominant
Tuvalu, Marshall Islands, Kiribati, and Vanuatu face existential erasure. Some low-lying islands have already been abandoned. At 5.1mm/year, these nations will be largely uninhabitable before 2060. Tuvalu has pre-negotiated citizenship rights with New Zealand — the world's first climate refugee treaty.
